Miso Soup

Description

Miso Soup is a heartwarming Japanese soup that is easy to make with a few healthy ingredients! It is the ultimate comfort food and pairs well with a variety of dishes.

Ingredients:

  • Miso past (red or white)
  • Dashi stock (dried konbu, dried bonito flakes)
  • Tofu
  • Green onions
  • Dry wakame seaweed (optional)

Steps:

Make Dashi Stock

  1. Rehydrate your dried wakame by soaking under one cup of warm water for 10 minutes. Drain well.
  2. Finely slice green onions.
  3. Cut tofu into bite-sized cubes.
  4. Add konbu and water into pot. Six grams of konbu and two cups of water should make two servings of miso soup. Place pot over medium-low heat.
  5. When liquid is simmering, turn off the heat.
  6. Submerge 7.5 grams of dried bonito flakes into the stock. Close pot with lid and steep the flakes for 7-10 minutes.
  7. Afterwards, remove konbu and bonito flakes with a fine-mesh strainer. Do not throw these away, as you can use them for other recipes and are edible by themselves.

Add Tofu and Wakame

  1. Add your bite-sized tofu to the pot. Bring stock back to a simmer over medium heat.
  2. Once stock starts to simmer, turn off the heat. Add the rehydrated wakame into the stock.

Mix Miso Paste into Dashi

  1. Add miso paste into the stock. 1 to 2 tbs should be enough, but add according to taste.
  2. Once everything is properly seasoned, add the green onions as garnish and enjoy!
